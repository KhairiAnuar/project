On board the USS Ronald Reagan during military exercise Talisman Sabre

The plane's pilot, Lieutenant Commander Andrew Dumm brings us in for a text-book arrested landing on the flight deck of US Navy aircraft carrier, USS Ronald Reagan

The plane he crews is one of about 60 fixed wing and rotary aircraft in the USS Ronald Reagan's armament

It's part of exercise Talisman Sabre, the largest joint military exercise ever held between Australia and the United States

He's served 33 years in the US Navy but this is his first Talisman Sabre exercise

Every day of the exercise up to 100 fighter jets, electronic attack, cargo and surveillance aircraft take off from the ship's flight deck

The USS Ronald Reagan is forward deployed in Japan at Yokosuka, while the airwing contingent is based in Atsugi and Iwakuni

The USS Ronald Reagan is now in its 17th year of service, and while the first of the new, Ford-class aircraft carrier has now been commissioned, Rear Admiral Thomas says the Nimitz will remain relevant
